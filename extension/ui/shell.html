🌀 What bakes here — Strudel → Ableton MIDI

Pitch & octave

note("c3 e3 g3") — note names or MIDI numbers 0–127
⚠ Strudel c4 = Ableton's “C3” (Strudel octave numbers run one higher). note("c4") lands on Ableton's middle C.
✗ .octave(n) is ignored here — shift with .add(note(12)).

Scales & keys

n("0 1 2 3 4 5 6 7").scale("C:major") → C…C (degree 7 = next octave)
default octave 3 (= Ableton “C2”). Raise: scale("C4:major") or .add(note(12))
per-bar key: n("0 2 4").scale("<C:major A:minor>")
modes: D:dorian, C:minor:pentatonic, …
⚠ n(…) with no .scale() = raw low MIDI — always add a scale.

Chords

stack: note("c,e,g") · n("0,2,4").scale("C:major")
voiced symbols: chord("Cm7").voicing() · "<C^7 Dm7 G7>".voicing()

Arpeggios

chord("C").voicing().arp("0 1 2 1") — numeric indices on a voiced chord
by hand: n("0 2 4 2").scale("C:major")
✗ named .arp("up") bakes nothing — use indices

Transpose

octave .add(note(12)) · semitones .add(note(7)) · .transpose(7)
✗ bare .add(12) is a no-op — wrap in note()

Velocity & probability → MIDI

.velocity(0.8) / .gain(0.8) (0–1 → 1–127)
.prob(0.7) / .chance(0.7) (0–1) — Live re-rolls each loop, so the clip evolves as it plays

Drums

s("bd sd hh oh") → Drum Rack pads (GM: bd = C1 = 36). Put a Drum Rack on the track.

Rhythm (mini-notation)

a*2 a/2 ~ rest a@3 hold a!2 repeat [a b] <a b> per-cycle a(3,8) euclid
.fast(2) .slow(2) .rev() .jux(rev) .every(4, fast(2)) .degradeBy(.3)
